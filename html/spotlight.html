The Engineer Behind the Code

On building systems she actually wants to use, finding the love of her life in AI, and why she can tell the difference between an ML engineer and someone who just calls an API

Interview by The Portfolio Magazine
April 2026

Quick Facts

  • Education BSc in Informatics Engineering, ISEP
  • Specialization Machine Learning
  • Location Porto, Portugal
  • Languages Portuguese, English

Susana Loureiro is 21, graduating in June, and already has a portfolio that most engineers twice her age would struggle to match. Three AWS certifications (AI Foundational, ML Engineer, Solutions Architect Associate) sit alongside projects she built not because a brief told her to, but because she genuinely wanted them to exist. She is precise about what she is and what she isn't, and she is not particularly interested in pretending otherwise.

You're graduating in June. How would you describe what kind of engineer you are?
The kind that wants to make something new and different. Something that actually solves a real problem, ideally one of mine. I need it to be functional, not just clever. I'm not building things to show off a technique. I want it to work and I want it to matter.
Your projects are very specific — fashion, people-counting, sentiment analysis. Where do the ideas come from?
The people-counting one was an internship project, so that was a company need. But the fashion projects..those came from me. I always wished I had a personal stylist. Someone to just tell me what to wear when I'm not in the mood to figure it out, which is almost all the time. So I built one.
I always wished I had a personal stylist. So I built one.
The AWS certifications came before the ML projects. Was that a deliberate strategy?
Not really. I didn't know what area of informatics I wanted to be in. Someone recommended AWS certifications, and I chose the AI ones because something about that direction appealed to me, even if I couldn't fully explain it at the time. I just followed the instinct.
At what point did it stop feeling like learning and start feeling like a vocation?
Halfway through studying for the AI Foundational. I always liked software engineering, there's a reason I chose this degree. But I never felt like it was the love of my life. Then I started going deep into AI and it was exactly that. I use that analogy deliberately. I like building systems. I love building intelligent ones. That distinction matters to me.
I like building systems. I love building intelligent ones.
You built a transformer from scratch in PyTorch rather than just using a pre-built model. Why go that deep?
Because that's the difference between an ML engineer and an AI engineer. An AI engineer uses models. An ML engineer builds them. If you don't understand what's happening underneath, you're not engineering, you're just calling an API. I want to actually know what I'm doing.
Where do you want to be in two or three years?
Full ML engineer. And very possibly with enough experience by then to have my own company. My ultimate life goal, I believe, is to make her. an actual product rather than a spare project. That's the goal. I'm not in a rush, but I'm not not thinking about it either.
